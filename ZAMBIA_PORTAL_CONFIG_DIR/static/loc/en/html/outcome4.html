Outcome 4. National REDD+ strategies identified

According to the Zambia UN-REDD Programme document Community-based REDD+ strategies need to be developed to counteract the drivers of deforestation. The envisaged outputs under Outcome 4 from the project document were as follows:

Output 4.1: Drivers of deforestation and forest degradation assessed. This output aimed to identify the drivers of deforestation and forest degradation.

Output 4.2: Candidate activities for REDD+ identified. This output aimed to identify appropriate activities for shifting forest resource use and thereby reducing deforestation and forest degradation. The suggested activities were:

  • Identify global best practices and benchmarking for forest management and REDD+ activities, and tailor practices to Zambian conditions to ensure the identification of suitable activities.
  • Identify and map multiple benefits of REDD+ implementation.
  • Identify evidence-based alternative livelihood options under REDD+.
  • Consult experts on the development of a framework addressing opportunity costs for key stakeholders as well as cost abatement curves and follow up actions.

Progress

A number of assessments, data collections and analytical works have been completed under Outcome 4; they will provide the basis to identify the REDD+ strategic options during 2013.

Analytical works under Output 4.1 and 4.2 were completed during 2012;

  1. Preliminary Drivers of deforestation & potential for REDD+ in Zambia. This study identified the prevailing drivers of deforestation and forest degradation in general terms in Zambia.
  2. Forest Management Practices with Potential for REDD+ in Zambia. This study identified global best practices and benchmarking for forest management and REDD+ activities, and tailor practices to Zambian conditions to ensure the identification of suitable activities.

Policy briefs were also generated during 2012;

  1. Policy Brief: Preliminary study on drivers of deforestation & potential for REDD+ in Zambia
  2. Policy Brief: Forest Management Practices with Potential for REDD+ in Zambia

During 2013 further analytical works have been launched and are close to completion;

  1. Drivers of deforestation, identification of threatened forests and forest co-benefits other than carbon from REDD+ implementation in Zambia. This study has produced a detailed and spatially-explicit analysis of drivers of deforestation and degradation. It also Identified and mapped the multiple benefits of REDD+ implementation.
  2. The Economic Context of Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and forest Degradation and the role of conservation, sustainable management of forests and enhancement of forest carbon stocks (REDD+) in Zambia. This study Identified evidence-based alternative livelihood options under REDD+, and also developed a framework addressing opportunity costs associated with the drivers of deforestation.

With the completion of analytical work in 2013 associated with a detailed study of drivers of deforestation and the economics (opportunity cost) of REDD+ and envisaged work under Outcome 4 will have been completed with the identification of National REDD+ strategic options.
